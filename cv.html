Research & Projects
Human Factors in Aviation Safety: Bridging Behavioral Science and Operational Practice
ISASI Forum — Journal of the International Society of Air Safety Investigators · Vol. 57, No. 2
2024 · Published
Published peer-reviewed article examining how behavioral science frameworks — organizational behavior and consumer behavior — apply to aviation human factors and safety investigations. Conducted in collaboration with the ISASI Human Factors Working Group and USC Viterbi Aviation Safety & Security Program. Study of 127 aviation safety experts worldwide. Available: lnkd.in/gT7ByF_Z
Trust-Centered Investigations: Integrating Human Factors and Behavioral Science with AI in Aviation Safety
Working paper · ISASI Human Factors Working Group · N=540 · 47 countries
2024–2025
Working paper. Multi-level study of 540 aviation safety professionals across 47 countries — 269 for individual-level SEM (EFA→CFA→SEM), 172 for organizational analysis. Primary finding: professional engagement explains 48.9% of AI trust variance (β = 0.727, p<0.001). Introduces the AI Trust Assessment Index (ATAI; α=.96, CR=.92). Key findings: HF maturity paradox (β = −0.11, informed skepticism vs. resistance), mid-career advantage (74% stronger engagement-trust relationship at 3–10 years), organizational threshold at integration level 3.5. Presented at ISASI Annual Seminar 2024 (Lisbon) and ISASI Annual Seminar 2025 (Denver).
