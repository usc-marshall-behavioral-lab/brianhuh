The Lab

The Student Research Assistant Pool Program

The Marshall Behavioral Research Lab maintains an active pool of undergraduate and graduate Research Assistants who support faculty study teams across the school. Brian manages and works directly with all of them — not as a program director at a remove, but as a hands-on staff researcher who is present in the work. Every RA gets one-on-one mentorship as a natural consequence of how the lab operates.

This is not a structured curriculum or external coaching arrangement. It is real research, running in a real lab, supervised directly by someone who has been doing this for eight years. Students who do this work learn what empirical behavioral research actually looks and feels like — the design decisions, the messiness of data, and the standards that separate rigorous work from noise.

What RAs actually do

01
Run live studies
RAs staff and execute in-person behavioral studies — managing participants, operating equipment, maintaining protocol fidelity, and handling data collection in real time. This is direct, hands-on research experience that classroom work cannot replicate.
02
Handle real data
Data cleaning, quality checks, flagging anomalies, preparing datasets for analysis. Students learn what real data looks like — which is messier and more demanding than textbook examples.
03
Learn research design
Working alongside faculty study teams, RAs see how studies are designed, what tradeoffs go into methodological choices, and what makes a study rigorous enough to produce interpretable results.
04
Develop technical skills
Qualtrics survey design, eye-tracking operation (Tobii), psychophysiology systems (BIOPAC), SONA participant management, data pipelines. Practical skills with real tools used in active research.
05
Work across 11 departments
The lab serves the full range of Marshall's academic units — RAs encounter research questions from marketing, organizational behavior, finance, management, and accounting, among others. Breadth is built in.
06
Receive direct mentorship
Brian works directly with every RA — on the research, on professional development, on understanding what they are doing and why. This is not mentorship at a remove. It is built into the daily structure of how the lab runs.
